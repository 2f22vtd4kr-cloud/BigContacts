Honest assessment, no flattery:
7.5 / 10

What genuinely earns that score:
* Architecture is legitimately sophisticated. BM25 + TF-IDF + all-MiniLM semantic + graph traversal + UCT + Bayesian-UCB as a 5-layer pipeline with no external AI API is real engineering, not a wrapper around GPT.
* Zero synthetic data is a rare discipline and makes every number in the UI trustworthy. Most "intelligence" tools fail this immediately.
* Persistence resilience — dual-Upstash design (dedup + contact cache) surviving GitHub imports and DB resets is elegant and operationally mature.
* Recurring scheduler running 7 jobs forever after boot means the app is genuinely always searching, not just processing a one-shot batch.
* UI is clean, professional, and all pages serve a purpose. The CRM pipeline, Outreach Assistant, Intel Terminal, and Persona Loop page are all real operator tools.

What holds it back:
* Contact hit rate: 2.3% — 729 contactable out of 32,101 entities. Most profiles are "registry trace only." The social-discovery and messenger-discovery modules are correct in design but FAA/HMLR registrants (corporate LLCs, property addresses) are not the same people as the HNWIs — so the enrichment often fires at a company name, not a person.
* Graph edge quality — 230k edges sounds impressive but the majority are CORPORATE_SERIES (name-token clustering) and FAA geo-proximity, not warm-introduction paths. The UCT path-finder works, but most paths dead-end at registrations rather than people you can actually reach through.
* Duplicate surface — "Riley Jacob" appeared twice on the dashboard today. The semantic dedup found 0 LIKELY_SAME_PERSON edges across 67k comparisons, which suggests the similarity threshold is too conservative.
* Data quality gap — FAA entities are often registered to aviation LLCs ("John Smith Aviation LLC"), not the individual. You're enriching the LLC, not the person. This is a structural limitation of the FAA source that no amount of enrichment fixes.

What would push it to 9+:
1. A people-resolution layer that maps LLC → beneficial owner before enriching (SEC Schedule 13D/G does this partially but you'd need more cross-referencing).
2. Higher-confidence Telegram/LinkedIn yield — the current pattern-guessing approach hits ~1–3% of targets. A curated list of known HNWI usernames or a paid data source for the top 1,000 targets would change that dramatically.
3. Tighter dedup — the semantic similarity threshold should be tuned so FAA "SMITH JOHN AVIATION LLC" and EDGAR "John Smith" collapse to one person where the evidence supports it.
Solid private tool with genuine technical ambition. The foundation is the right one — it just needs more targeted data coverage to reach its potential.
